Project Overview

Solving the Single-Source
Energy Dependency

Traditional water geysers depend entirely on one energy source — either electricity or LPG. When that source fails, hot water becomes completely unavailable. Frequent power cuts disable electric geysers, while LPG depletion halts gas-based systems entirely.

This project tackles that fundamental limitation by integrating both LPG and electricity into a single intelligent system. The hybrid controller monitors energy availability and automatically switches between sources — ensuring uninterrupted service even in regions with frequent power outages or irregular gas supply.

An interlocking mechanism guarantees only one heating mode operates at a time, preventing unsafe simultaneous operation. Combined with advanced thermal insulation (rock wool + ceramic fiber), the system minimises heat loss and reduces overall energy consumption.

Keywords: Hybrid water geyser · LPG and Electricity · Automatic source switching · Reliability · Temperature control · Gas solenoid valve · Water level sensor · Pressure relief valve · Energy conservation

Key Features

  • Automatic LPG ↔ Electric source switching
  • Water-level sensor — prevents dry heating
  • Temperature sensor + thermostat control
  • Gas solenoid valve — safe LPG control
  • Pressure relief valve (PRV) protection
  • Rock wool + ceramic fiber insulation
  • Arduino UNO based hybrid controller
  • SSR 40A for high-power AC switching
  • LED display — real-time temp & mode status
  • MCB — overload/short-circuit protection

System Architecture

Components & Structure

🔧
01
Storage Tank
Corrosion-resistant insulated tank. Central unit where water is stored and heated.
02
Electric Immersion Heater (3 kW)
Provides controlled, uniform heating in electric mode. Energised via SSR relay.
03
LPG Burner
High thermal output direct-flame burner below tank. Used for rapid, fast heating.
04
Gas Solenoid Valve
Controls LPG flow. Opens for LPG mode; auto-closes at target temp or under unsafe conditions.
05
Arduino UNO
Brain of the system — reads sensors, decides mode, controls SSR and solenoid valve.
06
Water Level Sensor (Float)
Verifies sufficient water before any heating. Prevents dry-run damage to heater and burner.
07
Temp Sensor (DS18B20)
Waterproof probe. Measures water temperature and sends digital signal to Arduino.
08
Thermocouple + Amplifier
Secondary temp sensing module. Sends temperature data to Arduino for monitoring.
09
Thermostat + Thermal Fuse
Thermostat cuts OFF at preset temp. Thermal fuse adds extra overheating protection.
10
Pressure Relief Valve (PRV)
Releases excess tank pressure. Prevents tank damage, rupture, or explosion.
11
SSR 40A (Solid State Relay)
Electronic switch — no moving parts. Controls high-power AC heater from 4–32V DC input.
12
Heat Sink
Aluminium fins dissipate heat from SSR. Prevents overheating and extends component life.
13
MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker)
Protects heater and electronics from overload or short-circuit. Auto-trips on fault.
14
Heat Diffuser
Distributes LPG flame heat evenly across heating surface. Improves thermal efficiency.
15
LED Display
Shows current water temperature, active mode (LPG/Electric), and system ON/OFF status.
16
Toggle Switch
Manual ON/OFF or mode selection. Selects Electric / LPG / Auto mode.
17
Rock Wool / Ceramic Fiber
Tank insulation that reduces heat loss. Maintains water temperature longer between cycles.
18
Gas Flow Control + Exhaust
Controls LPG flame intensity. Exhaust & flue outlet safely discharges combustion gases.

Working Principle

System Methodology & Workflow

1. System ON

Hybrid controller activates. Performs safety checks before any heating begins.

2. Water Level — OK?

Water-level sensor checks sufficient water. If LOW → system turns OFF immediately. If OK → continue.

3. Check Source Availability

Controller checks availability of electricity and LPG. Determines which modes can be used.

4. Both Available? — User Selects

If both available → user selects preferred mode. If only one → auto-selected without user intervention.

5. Interlocking

Only ONE heating source operates at a time. Simultaneous LPG + Electric is prohibited.

6. Electric Mode

Immersion heater energised via SSR. Thermostat + DS18B20 monitor continuously. Auto-OFF at preset temperature.

7. LPG Mode

Solenoid valve opens; ignition activates burner. Heat diffuser distributes flame. Solenoid auto-closes at target temp.

8. Source Failure? → Auto Switch

If active source fails mid-cycle, controller auto-switches to backup. Heating continues uninterrupted.

9. Temperature Reached → Hot Water

On reaching target temperature, hot water flows through outlet. System enters standby.

10. System OFF

After heating cycle completes, system turns OFF automatically. Saves energy, extends life.

Fig. 2 — Temperature vs Time Analysis

LPG heats 25°C to ~65°C in just 3–3.5 minutes due to high thermal output. Electric (3 kW) takes ~6 min — slower but more controlled. Hybrid: choose LPG for speed, Electric for precision.

Fig. 3 — Energy Consumption vs Time

Electric mode consumes energy slowly and steadily — ideal for temperature maintenance. LPG has higher energy per minute but reaches target faster. Hybrid allows smart mode selection.

Fig. 4 — Availability vs Reliability

Electric-only: ~70%. LPG-only: ~75%. Hybrid: ~98% — automatic source switching eliminates single-source downtime. A 23–28% improvement over conventional systems.

Fig. 5 — Cost vs Heating Mode

LPG costs ≈ ₹2.0 vs ₹2.3 for electric (5L, 25→65°C). Small per-cycle difference, significant over daily use. Hybrid lets user always pick the cheaper available source.

Safety Systems

Multi-Layer Safety Architecture

💧

Water Level Sensor

Checks water before any heating. Prevents dry-run damage to heater coil and LPG burner.

🌡️

Temperature Sensor + Thermostat

Continuously monitors water. Auto-switches OFF at preset temperature. Prevents overheating.

🔒

Gas Solenoid Valve

Auto-closes LPG supply when temperature is reached, on system OFF, or under unsafe condition.

⚡

Pressure Relief Valve (PRV)

Releases excess tank pressure automatically. Prevents tank damage, rupture, or explosion.

🛡️

Thermal Fuse + MCB

Thermal fuse cuts power on extreme overheating. MCB trips on overload or short-circuit faults.

🔗

Interlocking Mechanism

Hardware interlock ensures LPG and Electric NEVER operate simultaneously — eliminating dual-source hazards.
