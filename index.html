Overview

What Is This Project?

A research and invention disclosure on a dual-energy water heating system designed and built at SVERI's College of Engineering.

This project presents the design and development of a dual-source hybrid water geyser that operates using both LPG and electricity to provide a reliable, safe, and energy-efficient hot-water solution.

Traditional geysers depend on a single energy source, making them unreliable during power cuts or gas shortages, and increasing operating costs. The proposed system integrates both energy sources into a single unit, allowing smooth switching based on availability and user preference.

The system includes safety features such as a water-level sensor, temperature sensor, thermostat, pressure relief valve, and gas solenoid valve. LPG handles fast heating via its high thermal output, while electricity maintains controlled temperature. Thermal insulation reduces heat loss throughout.

How It Works

System Workflow

A structured 12-step automated process from startup to hot water delivery.

01
System Start
Hybrid controller activates when system is switched ON. Safety checks begin immediately.
02
Water Level Check
Float sensor verifies sufficient water in tank. Low water level halts the system to prevent dry-heating damage.
03
Energy Source Availability Check
Controller checks if electricity and/or LPG are available for use.
04
Mode Selection
If both sources available, user selects preferred mode. If only one source available, system auto-selects it.
05
Heating Activated
Selected source (electric or LPG) begins heating. Interlocking ensures only one source runs at a time.
06
Temperature Monitoring
Sensors continuously monitor water temperature and report to controller.
07
Source Failure Detection
If active source fails (power cut or gas depletion), controller detects it instantly.
08
Auto Backup Switching
On failure, system automatically switches to the backup source ‚Äî no manual intervention needed.
09
Temperature Target Reached
Once preset temperature is achieved, heating stops automatically to save energy.
10
Hot Water Delivery
Heated water is delivered via outlet for domestic or commercial use.
11
System Standby / OFF
System enters standby after heating cycle. Turns OFF safely to prevent overheating and extend life.
12
LPG Exhaust Handling
Combustion gases safely discharged via exhaust and flue outlet built into system.

Conclusion

Final Thoughts

A smart, sustainable, and future-ready approach to modern water-heating needs.

The Dual-Source Hybrid Water Geyser project effectively tackles the typical issues encountered by conventional water heating systems. Traditional geysers rely on a single energy source, which frequently causes problems during gas shortages or power outages. By integrating electricity and LPG into a single, intelligent system, this project guarantees a steady supply of hot water regardless of which source is available.


The system is dependable, effective, and user-safe due to automatic switching, appropriate safety features, and efficient thermal insulation. Performance analysis, reliability comparison, and cost evaluation demonstrate that the hybrid system is more flexible and reliable than single-source geysers ‚Äî with ~98% availability vs ~70% for electric-only systems.


This project provides a straightforward but effective solution for home and small commercial applications, with increased availability, improved energy management, and practical viability. Overall, it represents a modern and intelligent approach toward safe, flexible, and efficient water-heating technology.
